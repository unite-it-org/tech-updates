Tech Updates – July 2025

YouTube introduces AI‑based age verification for U.S. users

YouTube now uses AI to infer users' ages in the U.S., using behaviors like viewing history and account activity. Teens flagged by the system must verify via ID or selfie, restricting ads and enabling content controls.

AI designs paint formulas to keep buildings 5–20 °C cooler

Using ML‐driven material design, researchers developed coatings that significantly reduce heat absorption. Urban buildings could save tens of thousands of kWh annually, reducing AC reliance.

U.S. lifts AI GPU export ban to China amid policy debate

Washington reversed a ban to allow Nvidia to resume GPU exports to China, while lawmakers remain divided. Chinese firms accelerate domestic AI hardware development.

Nvidia CEO: “AI is the greatest equalizer” and will create more millionaires than the internet

Jensen Huang emphasized AI's democratizing impact, predicting frequent billion-dollar outcomes—even from small teams using generative tools to build world-changing businesses.

Gujarat government rolls out five‑year action plan to scale AI in governance

The state plans to train over 250,000 people—including MSMEs and students—and establish AI infrastructure to drive smart governance, research, and startups.

Fusion AI Summit to be held in Visakhapatnam, India, July 25–26

The summit will feature discussions on agentic AI, generative systems, edge and cloud AI, hyperautomation, and will engage startups, academia and investors in India's deep‑tech growth.

Microsoft outlines 40 job roles most Exposed to AI disruption

Roles like translators, writers, and sales representatives are identified at high risk as generative AI adoption rises. Entry-level positions face the greatest impact.

Quantum‑ML breakthrough improves semiconductor modeling for the first time

Researchers used a hybrid quantum‑classical model (QKAR) to predict chip performance from limited data, outperforming classical ML in semiconductor manufacturing.

Major model updates: Gemini Batch‑Mode API, Grok 4, Claude 3 and Copilot advances

July saw Google’s Batch‑Mode API for Gemini, xAI releasing Grok 4 for in‑car copilots, Anthropic launching Claude 3, Microsoft Copilot going GA across Office 365, and more.

Alibaba launches Qwen3‑Coder AI model as part of enterprise automation push

Qwen3‑Coder is Alibaba’s new coding AI aimed at boosting developer productivity. Alongside OpenAI, Microsoft and Google, Alibaba is scaling enterprise AI automation.
